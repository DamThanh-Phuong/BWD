QUANG NAM

Mi Quang

When you come to Quang Nam, you won't find it difficult to come across a Quang noodle shop at every corner. Quang noodles here are as popular as pho in Hanoi. In addition to the main ingredients of thin sliced rice crackers and broth, the specialty of Quang Nam also comes with chicken, snakehead fish, eel, beef, shrimp, frog... Grilled rice crackers, peanuts, and various fresh herbs enhance the flavor of the dish, stimulating the taste buds of the diners. Read more

Cao Lau

When visiting Quang Nam, sitting on a high floor to eat "Cao Lau" is a phrase that tourists often hear. Cao Lau shares many similarities with Quang-style noodles, but the preparation method is more elaborate. In the dish of Cao Lau, the noodles are crispy fried, and the broth is replaced with a little sauce. Cao Lau is usually served with a piece of Cao Lau, crispy fried pork skin, green vegetables, bitter vegetables, and peanuts... Read more

Banh Trang

Rice paper rolls with pork are so popular that it's not difficult for you to enjoy this dish in big cities like Hanoi, Saigon. But when you come to Quang Nam, try this dish once in its "hometown" and see how you like it! Dry rice paper is the authentic Dai Loc rice paper. Wet rice paper must be freshly made on the day. Pork belly must have fat on both ends. Enjoy it with fresh Tra Que vegetables... Read more

Nem Nuong

Grilled pork rolls are available in many regions, but the grilled pork rolls here have their own unique flavor - enticing enough to become a specialty of Quang Nam. Grilled pork rolls are made from lean meat mixed with fat and spices that are just right. When grilled on charcoal, the pork rolls ooze fat and emit an irresistible aroma... Read more

Banh Canh

The Banh Canh noodles are made from sticky rice, which does not break apart when boiled in water. Depending on the traditional secret recipes of each eatery, Banh Canh is prepared with various ingredients such as pork hock, snakehead fish, shrimp, pork sausage, crab... When enjoying it, you will feel the chewy texture of the noodles blending with the rich flavor of the broth, making you want to have another bite. Read more

QUANG NGAI

Don

Don is one of the famous specialty dishes that you cannot miss. Don is a species of mollusk, light yellow in color, as large as a little finger, with thin shell, only as big as a pinky finger and lives in the Ve River and Tra River area. After being boiled, Don meat is stir-fried with onions, minced garlic, and ground pepper. The best Don are boiled in sweet and clear broth, served with crispy grilled rice paper. The refreshing taste of the broth, combined with the sweetness of Don meat, brings a very attractive flavor. Read more

Banh Xeo

Unlike the northern or western-style sizzling pancakes, Quang Ngai sizzling pancakes are smaller and thinner, and are crispy or soft depending on the customer's preference. The main ingredients for making sizzling pancakes are rice flour, with fillings such as shrimp, bean sprouts, wood ear mushrooms, or minced duck meat. When served with cucumber, bitter banana, lettuce, fish mint, aromatic herbs, chives... and especially the indispensable pure fish sauce mixed with chili and garlic from Ly Son. Read more

Ca Bong

When it comes to Quang Ngai's specialties bought as gifts, you definitely can't overlook the Tra River carp. Carp is a gift favored by nature for the land of Quang Ngai, with the highest concentration in the heart of the Tra River. After being caught, the carp is simmered with fish sauce and sugar until it becomes firm and fragrant, adding a bit of pepper and chili on top makes it even more appealing. The most delicious Tra River carp is when enjoyed with a hot bowl of white rice. Read more

Cum Num

Another specialty of Quang Ngai that makes it hard for tourists to resist is the Sa Huynh crab. The Sa Huynh crab looks similar to a normal crab and is very nutritious. After being caught, they are cleaned and then steamed or grilled with various spices to bring a delicious and attractive flavor. Read more

Oc Hut

Oc Hut is not only a delicious snack but also a pride of the people of Quang region. This rustic dish has become a famous specialty, making foreign visitors every time they come here have to find and enjoy it. The dish is even more delicious when served with star fruit, laksa leaves, grilled rice paper or ginger fish sauce. The chewy, crunchy texture of the snail meat combined with a bit of spicy chili, the fragrant aroma of lemongrass helps "ignite" the taste buds. Read more

KON TUM

Pho Kho

Guests will find it extremely interesting to enjoy the dry pho dish in Kon Tum. Dry pho consists of 2 parts; a bowl of dry pho noodles with chewy and crispy strands, and a bowl of broth simmered from pork, chicken, and beef bones. Served with soy sauce dip and fresh, refreshing herbs. Just hearing about it is already enticing, isn't it! Read more

Heo Quay

Mang Den pig roast will be a specialty of Kon Tum that many tourists will enjoy tasting. The pigs here are all free-range, naturally raised, so they eat a variety of greens, pigs move and exercise daily so the meat is quite firm and different from regular pigs. When grilled, it has a delicious flavor, both chewy and tender, so you absolutely must try it. Read more

Ca Goi Kien Vang

It would be a great loss if the dish of golden ant salad fish is not included in the list of specialties of Kon Tum. This is a famous dish of the Rơ Măm people. The golden ant salad fish uses ingredients from large stream fish combined with young golden ants, if lucky enough to encounter ants with eggs, the dish will be extremely delicious. This dish is often served with sung leaves. It is both sweet and spicy, so it is a popular choice for many tourists. Read more

Bun Do

The red vermicelli noodles have the red color of cashews, tomatoes, the brown color of fish cakes, crab paste, and the white color of boiled quail eggs. Red highland vermicelli has a very delicious taste, you should try it. The flavors of the ingredients blend together to create a dish that is characteristic of this region. When traveling, remember to try this dish, it is perfect for breakfast and enjoying with coffee. Read more

Goi La

Goi La with the distinctive flavors of various types of leaves harmonizing with a well-balanced dipping sauce that is sour, bitter, spicy, and rich. With up to 30 types of greens for you to freely choose from, some unique to the Kon Tum region such as giant rice paddy herb, betel leaf, bear's garlic, wild betel leaf, red leafy branch,... or more common ones like water spinach, water morning glory, mango leaf, starfruit leaf, fig leaf, lotus leaf, guava leaf, mustard leaf, horseshoe crab leaf, perilla leaf,... Read more

GIA LAI

Pho Hai To

Pho two bowls or also known as dry pho. The dish is served in 2 bowls, 1 bowl of pho noodles and 1 bowl of broth. The pho noodles are made from ground rice flour then processed into small fibers. Gia Lai noodles are soft, chewy, not clumped together, not mushy. The noodles are served with shredded chicken, minced pork, beef tendon, beef shank or beef balls, and fried shallots. The broth is simmered from chicken or beef depending on each restaurant's secret recipe. Read more

Bun Mam Cua

Bun Mam Cua, also known as fermented crab noodle soup, is one of the most intriguing specialties of Gia Lai that piques the curiosity of visitors. The fermented paste used to make the broth is made from field crabs, giving it a distinctive and unique flavor. The noodles are served with crispy fried pork skin, shrimp crackers, pork sausage, and pork belly. While some may be hesitant at the unfamiliar smell of fermented crab, once they have tasted it, they will never forget it. Read more

Lau La Rung

In one of the regions with vast lush primeval forests, it is not difficult to find various types of wild vegetables. It is not just the flavor of each leaf, but also the distinctive taste that nature has bestowed upon this homeland. The forest leaves must be carefully picked and selected, they must be fresh and free of toxins, without causing harmful reactions. Not everyone can have the experience of picking wild leaves, as it is an art passed down by the ancients. Read more

BINH DINH

Goi Ca Chinh

Binh Dinh anchovy has a small, long body and uniquely sweet flesh. Slices of fresh anchovy mixed with raw vegetables accompanied by a sweet and sour taste, when eating, you should be careful, or you might get addicted. This dish tastes better when eaten on the spot, so you should consider when wanting to buy it as a gift. Read more

Mam Nhum

Nhum has many different types, but the Mam Nhum in Binh Dinh is especially made from the local nhum fish, creating a delicious flavor that goes well with any dish. At the same time, Mam Nhum in My An was once a specialty of Binh Dinh that was favored by the royal court, and now it is a dish that tourists cannot miss when visiting Binh Dinh. Read more

Muc Rim

Grilled squid is a popular dish that seafood lovers enjoy in most coastal areas. However, Binh Dinh-style grilled squid, also known as Binh Dinh-style dried squid, has a delicious aroma unique to the coastal region of Central Vietnam. The Quy Nhon-style grilled squid is made by locals using the freshest squid, and its flavor is unmatched anywhere else. Read more

Cha Tre

With its unique and exotic name, the dish Cha Tre, a specialty of Binh Dinh province, has sparked curiosity among many tourists who want to explore and taste this unique dish. Although it is widely available throughout the central region of Vietnam, the most delicious flavor is still found in Chả Tré Binh Dinh, made only with the traditional method and recipe unique to the land of Binh Dinh. Read more

Nem Cho Huyen

Nem Cho Huyen is a specialty of Binh Dinh province, extremely famous for its unique flavor, not as soft as Thu Duc Nem and not as sweet as Lai Vung Nem or An Cuu Nem. The Nem from this land has a chewy, crispy texture, with a captivating sour and sweet taste. The Nem filling is pink, wrapped in guava leaves and outermost layer is fresh green banana leaves. Nem Cho Huyen is fragrant when grilled over charcoal and served with coriander, perilla, and dipping sauce. In addition, Huyen sausage is also rolled with rice paper along with vegetables and fried sausage. Read more

PHU YEN

Mat Ca Ngu

Tuna's oceanic eyes are a dish that you can find all over Phu Yen, from high-end restaurants to budget eateries. The most common way of cooking is steaming or simmering softly with Chinese herbs, spices, garlic, onions, chili... Each portion of oceanic tuna eyes simmered with Chinese herbs costs around 40,000 VND, including 1 to 2 eyes - with a size equivalent to a rice bowl or larger. This is a nutritious dish like medicine, favored by both locals and distant travelers in Phu Yen. Read more

Chao Hau

A must-try street food specialty in this region is the clam porridge. The porridge is cooked deliciously, bringing a refreshing taste. Carefully selected clams, combined with porridge, create a rich and enticing bowl of porridge. The excellent taste of this dish is not only delicious but also very nutritious for human health. The perfect combination of smooth porridge and sprinkled spring onions creates an attractive color and adds a delicious aroma to the bowl of porridge. Read more

So Huyet

So Huyet have long been a familiar name on the map of Phu Yen's specialties, making everyone who comes here unable to resist tasting at least once. The So Huyet from this region exude a rich aroma, delicious and high-quality meat, a source of pride for the people of Phu Yen. Bun Bap

Bun Bap is famous for making the reputation of Phu Yen's specialty thanks to the delicious aroma it brings. The soft and chewy vermicelli combined with clear broth from pork, shrimp, and other seafood, creates a naturally sweet flavor. In addition, the dish is also combined with pork liver, snails, squid, and fresh herbs. If you want to explore the unique specialty of this land, you cannot miss this corn vermicelli dish! Read more

Bo Gu

"Bo Gu" is actually a fun name for the yellowfin tuna in Phu Yen. Just casually mentioning, there are grilled yellowfin tuna belly, yellowfin tuna egg salad, yellowfin tuna porridge, raw yellowfin tuna dipped in soy sauce (sashimi)...
